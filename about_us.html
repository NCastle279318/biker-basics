OUR HISTORY

Created at the turn of the Century by avid motorcyclist and former Traffic Officer, Lloyd Castle, the goal of the Academy is to provide motorcycle rider training in the form of Basic to Advanced level motorcycle handling and survival skills, of the kind employed by motorcycle police officers the world over, to the ever growing civilian motorcycle population.

The Academy opened its doors to its first civilian student rider back in 2001, prior to which these skills were exclusively taught to traffic and police motorcyclists and it has now trained literally thousands of riders since.

We have taught rider survival skills to riders who now enjoy motorcycling with the reduced risk that rider competence developed through proper training and practice allows.

OUR MOTIVATION

First hand experience of the carnage caused by untrained and ill-prepared riders gained from almost two decades of attending to collision scenes made it clear that law enforcement alone would not reduce the needless suffering.

It was clear that until formal motorcycle training was introduced to the general motorcycling population, many more would fall victim to their own lack of skill and inexperience.

The Academy was founded on meeting this need for structured formal motorcycle training and set out to provide the training required for survival.

OUR STAFF & CREDENTIALS

Lloyd Castle, a former trainer of Traffic Officers, with two decades of traffic safety and policing experience under his belt having served as a motorcycle officer on various specialised units has trained countless members of an array of law enforcement agencies since 1989.

Experience as a motorcycle patrol officer, a Presidential and VIP Motorcycle Escort as well as many years of experience in Collision Investigation and Reconstruction coupled with the insights gained from years of aerial observation of traffic movement and conflicts as a helicopter cop ensures that he is well qualified to teach the skills required to keep you safe.

His riding ability shows the depth of understanding of the vehicle dynamics of motorcycles he possesses.

His understated competence and quiet confidence, patience and evident ability to teach others allows you to come to that understanding too. He has more than 15 years practical experience as a motorcycle instructor for which he holds appropriate Certification.

Ruth Castle is the administrative head of the Academy. She runs the day to day administration of the enterprise and ensures that everything runs as it should.

Chat with her about all our courses or just get some advice, make a booking or discuss bikes or protective clothing and what is available in the market place in general.
